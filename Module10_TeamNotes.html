Module 10 | Team Notes & My Reflections

Unit 1: Delving into Scientific Investigation and Ethics

 

The unit has enabled me to explore the intricacies of research, scrutinizing a topic, unveiling discoveries, and unraveling the hows and whys of phenomena (Bailey et al., 2013). By introducing the scientific process, which entails keen observation, hypothesis formulation, justification, and testing, I can explore and apply the two main methods of problem-solving: deduction and induction (Miessler, 2020). Deduction involves moving from an idea to an observation, while induction moves from an observation to an idea. The paramount importance of research ethics is highlighted when it comes to including human subjects in research (Anderson & Hepburn, 2020). Therefore, as a student I have learnt to discern between inductive and deductive thinking and the profound significance of ethics in scientific inquiry (Mitchell, 2018).

 

Reference list

 

Bailey, M. et al. 2013. Applying Ethical Principles to Information and Communication Technology Research: A Companion to the Menlo Report

 

Anderson, H and Hepburn, B. 2020. 'Scientific Method' in Zalta, E. (Eds) The Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy (Winter 2020 edition). Metaphysics Research Lab, Stanford University.

 

Miessler, D. 2020. The Difference between Deductive and Inductive Reasoning.

 

 

Unit 2: Posing Research Questions, Conducting Literature Review, and Drafting Research Proposals

 

The unit focuses on crafting research questions and proposing research projects. I clearly understand that it is essential to have a clearly defined research topic to make research effective. A literature review is crucial in identifying relevant theories, approaches, and research gaps (Phillips & Pugh, 2010). Through the unit I have been able to  masters the art of writing and revising research questions, the elements of a research proposal, and the skill of presenting a comprehensive literature review. I am able able to identify appropriate research topics, transform research concepts into questions, and effectively give a literature review by the end of this unit.
 

Reference list

 

Phillips, E. & Pugh, D.2010. How to get a Ph.D.: A Handbook for Students and Supervisors. Open University Press.

 

 

Unit 3: Research Methods and Methodologies

 

The unit introduces various research methodologies and designs I can use as a researcher to develop new knowledge. Research design refers to the approach used to address a research topic, while research methodologies encompass systematic techniques for collecting, analyzing, and interpreting data (Sage, 2021). I have knowledge on two research designs covered, conclusive research, which validates conclusions and supports decision-making, and exploratory research, which focuses on a specific aspect of a study field. Qualitative, quantitative, and mixed methodologies are the three fundamental forms of research. Primary and secondary research techniques are also explored (Saunders et al., 2012). By the end of the unit, students will be familiar with several research methodologies and know which data-collecting strategies correspond to specific methodologies.

 

References list

 

Sage .2021. Sage Research Methods: Methods Map.

 

Saunders, M., Lewis, P. and Thornhill, A. 2012. Research Methods for Business Students 6th ed. Pearson Education Limited.

 

 

Unit 4: Case Studies, Focus Groups, and Observations
 

I have knowledge of various data-collecting techniques, including case studies, focus groups, and observations. While focus groups collect information from a selected sample of people, case studies involve in-depth investigations of specific individuals or groups (Hennink et al., 2020). Through the unit I have knowledge of the quantitative and qualitative observations, emphasizing numerical data and monitoring traits (QuestionPro, 2021). The unit emphasizes the importance of using various data-gathering techniques as the research project requires. By the end of this unit, students will be adept at using these data collection methods and will be able to determine which best suits their research project.

 

Reference List

 

Hennink, M., Hutter, I. and Bailey, A., 2020. Qualitative research methods. Sage.

QuestionPro. 2021. Questionnaire vs. Survey: Is there a Difference?

 

 

Unit 5: Interviews and Survey Methods

 

Through the unit I have vast knowledge of surveys as a potent quantitative tool and in-depth interviews as a prevalent qualitative research technique. Surveys scrutinize attitudes and trends, while in-depth interviews stimulate one-on-one discussions to extract comprehensive information (Rashid et al., 2019). Pre- and post-testing tactics are also encompassed for side-by-side comparisons. Surveys and interviews are frequently employed in tandem to amass more data. By the end of this lesson, I am able to elucidate how surveys, interviews, and pre-and post-testing may all be employed in research (Snyder, 2019).

 

References List

 

Rashid, Y., Rashid, A., Warraich, M. A., Sabir, S. S., & Waseem, A. (2019). Case study method: A step-by-step guide for business researchers. International journal of qualitative methods, 18, 1609406919862424.

 

Snyder, H. (2019). Literature review as a research methodology: An overview and guidelines. Journal of business research, 104, 333-339.

 

 

Unit 6: Questionnaire Design

 

The unit has been instrumental especially in increasing my knowledge on the questionnaire as a data collecting tool. The unit spotlights crafting questionnaires and clusters of questions utilized in surveys. Inquiries based on the questionnaires can be amassed from respondents in many ways using questionnaires, which provide swiftness (Busetto et al., 2020). The research methodology governs the questions, open and closed, that are often combined in the research process depending on the ability of the respondents to reply to questions. The unit encompasses techniques for obtaining answers, enhancing answers, and interpreting the data collected (Varghese et al., 2019). As a learner, I should be able to construct questionnaires, differentiate between a good questionnaire and a bad one, and comprehend data analysis after the unit.

 

Reference List

 

Busetto, L., Wick, W. and Gumbinger, C., 2020. How to use and assess qualitative research methods. Neurological Research and Practice, 2, pp.1-10.

 

Varghese, S. S., Ramesh, A., and Veeraiyan, D. N. 2019. Blended Module‐Based Teaching in Biostatistics and Research Methodology: A Retrospective Study with Postgraduate Dental Students. Journal of dental education, 83(4), 445-450.

 

 

Unit 7: Validity and Generalizability in Research

 

In Unit 7, I underwent a challenging experience where I flopped a literature review assignment and received no tutor feedback. The event has emphasized the importance of comprehending validity and generalizability in research, even though the text does not discuss specifics (Wan, 2022). Validity is the degree to which study findings are reliable and accurate, whereas generalizability is the degree to which results may be applied to a broader population. It reminds me of the significance of scrutinizing the caliber and relevance of literature sources attentively and considering how they may influence the reliability and applicability of my study (Bloomfield and Fisher, 2019).

 

Reference List

 

Varghese, S. S., Ramesh, A., and Veeraiyan, D. N. 2019. Blended Module‐Based Teaching in Biostatistics and Research Methodology: A Retrospective Study with Postgraduate Dental Students. Journal of dental education, 83(4), 445-450.

 

Bloomfield, J., and Fisher, M. J. 2019. Quantitative research design. Journal of the Australasian Rehabilitation Nurses Association, 22(2), 27-30.

 

 

Unit 8: Inferential Statistics

 

Unit 8 exposed me to diverse analyses for quantitative data and hypothesis testing, primarily focused on inferential statistics. I have now grasped that inferring reliable conclusions about a population from a sample is known as inference (Rahman, 2020). The unit has expanded my cognizance of applying statistical tools to derive findings, test theories, and interpret data meaningfully. It has underscored the importance of selecting the proper statistical tests, comprehending the underlying assumptions, and communicating the findings effectively to enable evidence-based decision-making (Mondal et al., 2022). Rigorous quantitative research will benefit from the knowledge and skills acquired in this section.
