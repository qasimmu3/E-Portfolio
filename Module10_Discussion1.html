Discussion Post # 1 – Initial Post - The Ethics of Autonomous Cars


The case study I have chosen from the Association of Computing Machinery (ACM) is "The Autonomous Car Dilemma," which raises ethical issues surrounding autonomous cars and the decisions they may have to make in potentially fatal situations (Brayshaw et al., 2020). The ACM Code of Ethics covers the scenario, which emphasizes the importance of safeguarding the well-being of those affected by computer technology. Principle 1.1 stipulates that computing professionals should "contribute to society and human well-being" by prioritizing safety and minimizing harm (Ladwig and Schwieger, 2020). The ethical difficulty in the case of autonomous cars lies in the split-second decisions that must be made in situations where harm is unavoidable. The code of ethics mandates that computer scientists involved in developing autonomous vehicle systems prioritize the safety of all involved and consider the potential consequences of their decisions. The focus on professional accountability and human well-being is mirrored in the British Computer Society's (BCS) Code of Conduct, which also emphasizes "Public Interest" and "Professional Competence and Integrity." The case study's implications for pertinent legal problems include accountability and responsibility for accidents or injuries brought on by autonomous cars (Saltz and Dewar, 2019). The deployment of autonomous vehicles poses ethical concerns concerning the general public's acceptance of and faith in the technology.


Computer experts' professionalism in developing and deploying autonomous cars is essential to keep the public's trust and ensure moral decision-making. I used the ACM Code of Ethics and the BCS Code of Conduct to substantiate my analysis (Ladwig and Schwieger, 2020). The publications offer a thorough foundation for moral behavior in the computing industries. The scholarly literature on ethics in autonomous cars also advanced my knowledge of the subject. Overall, examining this case study and using the codes of ethics gave us a critical new understanding of the moral issues facing autonomous cars. It emphasized putting human safety first, considering legal and societal ramifications, and maintaining professionalism in the computing industry (Lee, 2021).
